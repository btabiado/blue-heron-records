Meet the founder

Joe LeDuc

Founder · Producer · Promoter · Audio Engineer

Blue Heron Records begins and ends with Joe LeDuc. A lifelong music lover, Joe traded a career as a senior technical project manager for a second act doing what he loves most — discovering local artists and helping the world hear them.

From recording, mixing, and mastering in his home studio to taking the stage with the BHR House Band and the Lemont School of Rock adult band, Joe lives and breathes music every day. Equal parts producer, promoter, and biggest fan, he’s the heart and hustle behind every Blue Heron release.

Get to know us

A record company built around local artists

Blue Heron Records is a record company supporting local artists, built around a home recording and post-production studio in Oak Lawn, Illinois. We help musicians make great music — and we help the world hear it.

We’re proud to partner with Picture-Minded Studio — a state-of-the-art, 1,200 sq ft Dolby Atmos facility in Lemont, IL with audio engineers who know how to vibe.

Our home

A cozy home studio with a big heart

Our home-based studio in Oak Lawn has three small practice and recording rooms. Tucked between two railroad lines and the occasional Midway flight path, we’ll never pretend to be a fully sound-proofed commercial room — but there’s plenty of quiet to capture song demos and pre-album projects with real care.

We specialize in recording, mixing, and mastering on industry-standard Pro Tools Studio, plus keyboard, strings, choir, and horn post-processing to bring a song to life.

Our story

From a homespun studio to a real record company

Blue Heron Records was founded in late 2023 by Joe LeDuc — an aspiring artist promoter, record producer, and audio engineer whose homespun facility in Oak Lawn would go on to record a video Christmas special, song demos, host record-release parties, and even an all-day music festival featuring South Chicago locals.

In 2024, the label began promoting two local singer-songwriters and a couple of talented vocalists. We capped the year by officially becoming a record company — recording two original songs in the brand-new Picture-Minded Studio.

Many thanks to co-producers and musical artists Kurt Bonomo and Ben Kremer for helping make musical dreams a reality.

Six spaces, one creative home

Demo Studio

Powered by Pro Tools Studio, a deep library of virtual instruments, and KRK studio monitors and headphones.

Small Isolation Booth 100 sq ft

Studio resources

The gear behind the sound

Blue Heron Records is proud to make this recording and performance equipment available to BHR artists, collaborators, and local musicians.

Recording Equipment

Interface & Mixing
  • Focusrite Scarlett 4i4 (3rd Gen) — 4-in / 4-out USB interface, 24-bit/192kHz, Air-mode preamps.
  • Behringer Xenyx X1222USB — 16-input analog mixer with compressors, British EQs & 24-bit multi-FX.
Microphones
  • RØDE NT1 5th Gen — large-diaphragm condenser with onboard DSP, shock mount & pop filter.
  • Shure SM58-LC — the world-standard cardioid dynamic vocal mic.
  • Shure SM57 — the go-to dynamic for instruments and amps.
  • Sennheiser e835-S — rugged dynamic cardioid vocal mic.
  • Sennheiser e906 — flat-profile dynamic for amps, horns & percussion.
  • Cloudlifter CL-2 — 2-channel mic activator, +25 dB clean gain for passive & ribbon mics.
Monitoring
  • KRK Rokit RP5 G4 — 5" bi-amp powered studio monitors with DSP EQ.
  • KRK KNS 8400 — closed-back reference studio headphones.
Guitars & Bass
  • Squier Affinity Stratocaster HHS, Epiphone ES-339 semi-hollow & Squier Sonic P-Bass.
  • Early-2000s Mexican Telecaster, Fender Acoustasonic & a refinished ’70s Stratocaster.
  • Epiphone PR-350 all-solid acoustic, Orangewood Morgan Mahogany Live & a Kamoa ukulele.
Amplifiers
  • Positive Grid Spark — 40W smart amp & Bluetooth speaker.
  • Positive Grid 140W FRFR powered cabinet.
  • Fender J.A.M. — early-’90s USA 75W combo with onboard chorus.
  • Fender Frontman 10G — compact practice amp.
Keyboards & Synths
  • Moog Prodigy — classic analog monophonic synthesizer.
  • Casio WK-1630 — 76-key touch-sensitive workstation.
Pedalboard
  • Donner DP-1 power supply, Ultimate Comp 2, Blues Drive, Stylish Fuzz, Yellow Fall delay & Verb Square reverb.
  • BOSS DS-1 Distortion, BOSS AC-3 Acoustic Simulator, Nobels ODR-1 Natural Overdrive.
  • Dunlop Cry Baby Wah, Danelectro Fab Chorus & a Donner Deluxe Looper.

Performance Equipment

  • Bose L1 Pro16 — portable line-array PA with built-in mixer & Bluetooth.
  • Positive Grid Spark LIVE — 4-channel smart amp & PA system.
  • Alto Professional TX310 — 350W powered loudspeaker.
Performance Lighting
  • 4× 36-LED RGB PAR lights with DMX control, T-bar stand & gig bag.

Education

Our commitment to music education

We believe local music makers should be empowered to explore audio production as a hobby, a passion, or a career.

Home recording is easier and more affordable than ever — a mix of computers, industry-standard and open-source software, and vintage and modern microphones. That’s why we’re proud to attend and recommend audio-engineering classes, camps, and learning opportunities through our partnership with Picture-Minded Studio.

Learn about Picture-Minded Studio →

Many of our team members support and actively participate in the School of Rock ecosystem, so we’re thrilled to help interested musicians sharpen their skills. We’re proud to recommend and sponsor classes, camps, and opportunities through our partnership with School of Rock Lemont.

Special thanks

To our close collaborators

What we do wouldn’t be possible without the supporters and collaborators who work behind the scenes.

Barb “Wa” Zurek — for her undying support, a home for the BHR studio, and an undeniable ear for a hit song.

Kurt Bonomo at Player’s Guitar — who brought out the talents of our first two recording artists through instruction, multi-instrument tracking, and producing their first original songs. Thanks to Player’s Guitar for giving BHR a musical home in Worth and for decades of service to local, regional, and national musicians.

Ben Kremer at Picture-Minded Studio — for bringing his production, audio engineering, drumming, percussion, mixing, and mastering talents to our first record-company songs in 2024. We look forward to turning more music into loud-and-proud masters ready for streaming, CD, tape, or vinyl.

Braeden Janes at Picture-Minded Studio — for sharing his audio-engineering, post-production, and record-company wisdom, and for his help getting Blue Heron Records and our artists online.

Gary “Martin” Welk — my college roommate, who paid forward an acoustic guitar ten years ago with the words, “Now that I learned to play this guitar, you should next — you’re the one with musical skills.” Ha — I had him fooled. All of my past and current music instructors — who have patiently held my hand and fed my lifelong passion to appreciate and create music.
